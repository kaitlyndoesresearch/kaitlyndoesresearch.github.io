About Me

I am a third-year student in the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) graduate program at Iowa State University, but more than that, I am an intensely curious researcher with a penchant for creative expression. My current research interests center around the ways groups of people use emerging and extant technologies to enhance their social interaction, while previously I have also examined technology's place in learning, the usability of commercial products, and the use of virtual and augmented reality by older adults.

After learning about research through a lens of psychology and statistics as an undergraduate, I attained my M.S. in HCI in August of 2019, studying the impacts of a software program and several individual personality differences on the ability of a team in a military-style, computer-simulated task. To date, I have crafted twelve surveys and led seven semi-structured interviews. As a user-researcher I have implemented tools such as semi-structured interviews and well-crafted surveys in all nine projects I have been involved with, six of which are documented in this portfolio. Click here to learn more!

My main interest is in social interaction. I want to help teammates interact effectively whether they are solving problems in the same room or in the same application. Whereas mobile phones and video games sometimes have a bad wrap as disruptors of social interaction, I see a real opportunity for improving same-place, or co-located, socialization via technology, and I am determined to learn more about this possibility.

I have a passion for the creative, seeking fulfillment as a poet, a painter, and a non-fiction writer. The painting I am most proud of, currently, is pictured here! I also enjoy running and hiking, playing cooperative multi-player video games, traveling (internationally or locally) with my partner, and spending time with my two kitties.
